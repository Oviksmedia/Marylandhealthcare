## Brand Register

**Surface type:** Brand + Conversion. The design IS the product. It must communicate trust before it communicates anything else.

**Brand identity in one sentence:** Maryland Healthcare is the most trusted family hospital in Port Harcourt — not because they say so, but because they have been there, continuously, since 1982.

**The emotional truth:** When a patient lands on this site, they are asking one question before any other: *"Can I trust these people with my family?"* Every design decision — color, typography, image, copy — must answer yes before they scroll.

---

## The Brand Story

Maryland Healthcare was established on **May 1st, 1982** at #42 Igbokwe Street (Bekweri Wosu Street), Dline, Port Harcourt. They are now at 14 Chief Elechi Amadi Road, Rumuokwurushi. **44 years of continuous operation** in the same city. Their doctors know your name. Their staff has treated multiple generations of the same families.

This is not a startup. This is not a new clinic trying to look prestigious. This is an institution that has earned its place.

**The differentiator is not technology. It is relationship.** A patient testimonial says it directly: *"The staff actually knows your name."* That is the brand.

**The secondary differentiator is modern access.** They offer telemedicine — and for Port Harcourt, where traffic is genuine daily friction, *"Skip the waiting room"* is not a feature. It is relief.

## Users & Jobs-to-Be-Done

### User 1 — The Port Harcourt Family
- **Who:** Parents aged 30–55. Making healthcare decisions for children and aging parents.
- **Digital literacy:** Medium. Uses mobile primarily.
- **What they want:** A hospital that won't treat them like a number. Consistency. Someone who remembers their child's history.
- **JTBD:** *"When my family needs a doctor, I want to go somewhere I trust and where they know us, so I don't have to re-explain everything every time."*
- **Emotional trigger:** Recognition and continuity. "They've been here longer than I've been alive."

### User 2 — The Corporate / HMO Patient
- **Who:** Working professionals referred through AxaMansard, Solewant LLC, or another HMO.
- **Digital literacy:** High. Time-conscious.
- **What they want:** Confirmation that their company chose a quality hospital. Professional, efficient, credible.

### User 2.1 — The HMO Trust Section
- **Eyebrow:** THE MARYLAND NETWORK
- **Headline:** Accepted by 28+ leading HMOs and international partners.
- **Sub-text:** Because your corporate benefits should work seamlessly when your family needs them most.
- **Visual (Marquee):** A continuous, slow-moving horizontal scroll of high-contrast HMO logos (AxaMansard, Solewant, etc.) in grayscale, turning to full color on hover.
- **Purpose:** Immediate "institutional trust" signal.
- **JTBD:** *"When I need to use my HMO, I want a hospital that is professional and actually accepts my plan, so I feel my benefits are worth having."*
- **Emotional trigger:** Institutional credibility. Stats, specialties, years of operation.

### User 3 — The Telemedicine Seeker
- **Who:** Urban professional, 25–45, used to Lagos or Abuja-level digital convenience.
- **Digital literacy:** High. Port Harcourt traffic is a daily stress.
- **What they want:** Quality medical advice without leaving the house or fighting traffic.
- **JTBD:** *"When I need a quick consultation or prescription refill, I want to do it from my phone without wasting three hours in transit."*
- **Emotional trigger:** Time saved. "Smart people use this."

## Logo — Visual Identity Anchor

**Logo:** MHC monogram with heartbeat line. Two-tone teal: bright cyan-teal (M shape + slanted element) and dark slate-teal (H + C). Wordmark: "FAMILY HEALTHCARE" in near-black uppercase, spaced.

**What the logo locks in:**
- Primary colors MUST be teal-family. No forest green, no navy as primary.
- The heartbeat line signals clinical competence — it is the technical trust marker embedded in the brand mark.
- Near-black text for all body copy — matching the wordmark.
- The two-tone teal creates a built-in gradient/depth system to use throughout the interface.

## Anti-References — Do NOT Produce These

1. Generic blue-and-white hospital template (sterile, Bootstrap-looking)
2. Hero with a photo of medical equipment, stethoscopes, or empty hospital corridors
3. "Welcome to Maryland Healthcare" as an H1
4. Green medical cross as a primary visual motif
5. Stock photos of nurses pointing at clipboards or generic diverse-team shots
6. Carousel/slider in the hero
7. Equal visual weight for all services — Emergency must always be elevated
8. "Health, Elevated." or "Your Health, Our Priority" — generic prestige copywriting
9. Stark black editorial look (like a luxury consumer brand — wrong register for a hospital)
10. Anything that could be on ANY hospital website in the world — specificity is the antidote

## Design Principles

1. **Legacy Forward** — The 44-year history is the primary trust signal. It must appear above the fold, before the first scroll. Never bury it in a footer.

2. **Specificity Over Generics** — Real names (Dr. Stephen Ekwelibe), real addresses (14 Chief Elechi Amadi Road), real patient types (AxaMansard HMO). Generic hospitals say "experienced team." Maryland says the doctor's name.

3. **The Background Is The Argument** — Every hero-level image should be chosen for the emotional argument it makes, not for decoration. A doctor-patient consultation scene says "we are real and we care" before a single word is read.

4. **Telemedicine as Front Door** — Not just a listed service. It gets elevated treatment on the homepage — its own section with its own story. 

5. **Telemedicine Feature (Inverted)**
- **Background:** Deep Teal
- **Headline (Cream text):** "Your Doctor Is Always Within Reach."
- **Sub-text:** "Experience clinical excellence without the commute. No waiting rooms, no formalities—just a direct connection to a doctor who listens."
- **Visual:** Cinematic full-width (16:9) photo of a patient at home on a video call with a doctor.
- **Process Steps:**
  1. Consult Online
  2. Share Your Story
  3. Receive Expert Care
- **CTA:** "Book a Virtual Session".

5. **Emergency Always Accessible** — The emergency number is in the navigation, always visible, always in the coral accent color. A patient in crisis must never scroll to find it.

6. **Warmth Through Details** — The floating badge with the founding date and address. The doctor's portrait with a direct quote. The patient names in testimonials. Details create trust — placeholders destroy it.

7. **Mobile-First Behavior** — Port Harcourt users are predominantly on mobile. Layout decisions must always be validated at mobile first. No content hidden on mobile that matters on desktop.

---

## Accessibility

- **WCAG Level:** AA minimum
- **Contrast:** All text must pass 4.5:1 against its background
- **Focus states:** Visible custom focus rings on all interactive elements (no outline: none without replacement)
- **Semantic HTML:** Proper heading hierarchy h1 → h6 on every page. One h1 per page only.
- **Touch targets:** Minimum 44×44px on all interactive elements
- **Emergency CTA:** Must be reachable via keyboard in ≤ 3 tab stops from page load
